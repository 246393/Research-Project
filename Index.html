In-Ear

An in-ear headphone is any headphone that is inserted into the ear. Which would also include earbuds of both the wireless and wired types. The earbud sits at the ear cannel, providing a more isolated listening experience. With the added bonus of noise cancellation, Earbuds are as popular, if not more, as over-the-ear headphones. This includes hearing aids and IEMs. IEM stands for In-Ear Monitors, which would include all the earbuds singers and people in entertainment and news broadcasters wear.

Most in-ear headphones come wireless with the help of Bluetooth to conceal the appearance of their headphones. It is most commonly used during exercise due to its light weight. Some popular brands of earbuds are AirPod, Raycon, Galaxy Buds, and many more, since companies also sell other types of headphones along with the earbuds.

Fun fact, earbuds is one of the many names called for a headphone. Some popular colors for the earbuds are more vibrant in color than the other headphone counterparts. Includes: rose gold, royal blue, white, black, and gray.

On-ear

On-ear headphones are overlooked in the headphone world. When looking for headphones, people usually look for Over-ear headphones. There are some advantages of using an On-ear headphone. As the name states, the cups of the headphones sit on top of the ears of the user. Depending on the user, it can be more comfortable to use a smaller earcup. It can relieve some pressures when using over the ear headphones. Due to it's smaller nature of their cups, they are also lighter than over the ear headphones. The added bonus of the smaller nature of the headphones lets the user be able to hear the ambient sounds. Ambient sounds are importent in areas of heavy traffic and high population due to the need for alertness in the dense areas.

Due to the uptake of better noise cancelling from over the ear headphones, on-ear headphones are harder and harder to find. On-ear headphones comes in both wireless and wired versions. Some popular brands include SkullCandy, Jabra, Grado, and Senheiser. The smaller ear cups all come down to preference since some people say it's more comfortable to wear on-ear, while most disagree.

Over-Ear

When thinking about a headphones, the first image that comes to mind is most likely an Over-ear headphones. With its powerful noise-cancelling quality, it's getting more and more popular by time. While wireless are most popular, many artist use Over-ear headphones when creating their songs. The isolation of their song from the outside world is something they value greatly. As the name suggest, the headphone's cups sit over the user's ear creating a seal for the noise cancellation.

The benefits of an Over-ear headphone is comfort and noise cancellation. With comfort, since the cups of the headphones aren't directly on the ear, it takes off the pressure from the ear. The noise cancellation comes in active and passive versions. Passive noise cancellation is used to isolate irregular and high-frequency sounds. The benefits are that it's a phsical barrier that doesn't need power to function. Perfect for concerts and high volumes. Active, on the other hand, needs energy to work and targets ambient sounds which are the low to medium pitch range. This is the noise cancelling people think of where they just turn it on and their cafe turns quiet. Perfect for airplane trip with the constant chatters and hum from the plane. The headphone comes in all different types of colors. Popular colors include: red, black, silver, white and blue.
